
!!! tldr "Rule"
        Boldface is our primary means for emphasizing single terms in a Brilliant lesson.

The main use of boldface is to highlight the course-specific vocabulary needed to comprehend the lesson. Boldface text stands out from normal text. You can easily pick out boldface terms as you scroll through a block.

The first instance of a new piece of vocabulary should always be set in boldface, regardless of whether it is a [glossary term](glossary.md).

!!! check "Example"
    The combination of a car's speed and direction is called its **velocity**, represented in the figure by a yellow arrow.

Boldface can also be used to attract attention to essential words. In selecting words to bold, consider words would be easy to misread or gloss over otherwise. If the learner misses these words, they probably miss the question.

!!! check "Example"
        How many people do you expect will have a **positive** test result and **do not** have the disease?


An entire phrase in boldface is meaningless. Single terms — no more than a few words — are the largest unit of content to appear in boldface.
