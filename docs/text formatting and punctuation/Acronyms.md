# Acronyms

Learners make associations between ideas and vocabulary through multiple exposures. Acronyms are also established through repetition. For this reason, introducing an acronym that is used in a small number of lessons is not recommended.

The convention for introducing acronym

However, in some courses, certain compound nouns are central to the content. These terms are repeated many times across the course, like `artificial neural network` in Intro to Neural Networks, and `ordinary differential equation` in Differential Equations I.

In these instances, introducing an acronym (`ANN` and `ODE`) can condense text considerably, which is why we recommend it. However, it is important to establish the mapping between the term and the acronym in multiple lessons at the beginning of the course.

If you are unsure about using a particular acronym, start up a conversation about it in `#editorial-lounge`.
