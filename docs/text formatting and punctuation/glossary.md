---
title: Glossary Cards
---


!!! tldr "Rule"
    Glossary cards display the meanings of important terms. They are placed so that users don't need to navigate to an earlier block or lesson to look up the meaning of a term.

- Don't use a glossary card on the first use of a term in a course. The term should be formatted in boldface, and may appear in a concept box.
- When a term appears next, after the block in which it is first introduced, attach a glossary card to it with the Camperdown markup `[term](glossary "label for term")`
- The same glossary card should not appear more than once in the same block — learners can scroll up to find glossary cards. Too many glossary cards in a lesson feels clutter-y.
- Assume the learner needs to reference the glossary card in the first ~3 blocks in which the term is used.
- When a term is used in a subsequent lesson, the first occurrence should have a glossary card attached. You don't need to attach a card to subsequent occurrences in the same lesson.
- If a term is central to the course, and is used in nearly every lesson after it is introduced, stop attaching a glossary card to the first occurrence of the term after the third lesson after it was introduced.
