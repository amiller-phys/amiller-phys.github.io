# Sentence Smithing

!!!tldr "Rule"
    Chisel your sentences while preserving meaning

The first draft of a sentence is usually not the one you want to publish. There are bound to be extra words, or complex structure that can be edited out.

Don't wait around waiting for the most concise or elegant phrasing as you draft — write, slash, and wordsmith.

Once you're sure about what you want your sentence to communicate, look over what you've written. Does anything fails to contribute to that meaning? If so, it can be deleted.

The usual suspects include

- adverbs,
- multi-word phrases,
- extraneous details,
- phrases that are separated from the main clause by a comma.

Chisel until you can't remove any more without changing its meaning.
