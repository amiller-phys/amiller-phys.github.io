# Blue-Collar Writing

!!!tldr "Rule"
    Use the simplest possible words to express an idea — but no simpler.

A Brilliant lesson is not an academic paper or textbook.

Like a good newspaper, our writing informs a thoughtful, diverse, and interested audience. The words we choose make our ideas precise and memorable for this audience. If language gets in the way because it is ambiguous, or unfamiliar, or distracting, then it is not serving our purposes.

We use a simple vocabulary and direct sentences to express ideas. We call this **blue-collar writing** because our audience is wider than the audience of STEM textbooks.

Our words are almost always commonplace, but never boring. Use $5 words only if you need the specificity — if a more common word can achieve the same meaning, use the common one.

- Some words never pass this test, like "utilize".

!!! failure "No"
    When evaluating an expression with a repeated variable, we replace every occurrence of the variable with its specified value.

!!! success "Yes"
    Evaluating an expression means we replace every occurrence of the variable with its value.

- Language used in storytelling should be as direct as possible.

!!! failure "No"
    Frustrated with your inability to resolve the disputes with your friend, you return to playing with your Decision Box.

!!! success "Yes"
    Frustrated by the disagreements, you return to playing with your Decision Box.

- Avoid "math speak."

!!! failure "No"
    Given that each right triangle below is isosceles (the legs in each triangle have the same length), what is $x$?

!!! success "Yes"
    Each right triangle below is isosceles — the legs in each triangle have the same length. What is $x$?

- Avoid colloquialisms.

!!! failure "No"
    This is all well and good when there's no acceleration, but what do we do when there is?


!!! success "Yes"
    This is fine when there's no acceleration, but what's different when there is?


- Choose simple words that don't have multiple meanings.

!!! failure "No"
    Determining whether a sum converges or diverges is a subtle business, and a large chunk of calculus is devoted to finding convergence tests.

!!! success "Yes"
    It can be tricky to determine whether a sum converges or diverges. Convergence testing is a large portion of a calculus course.

- Be intentional about your word choices. Avoid repeating high-entropy words in nearby sentences (or the same sentence).

!!! failure "No"
    Their shared love of ice climbing brought them back **together** after years apart. Once they were **together**, they'd never be apart.

!!! success "Yes"
    Their shared love of ice climbing brought them back **together** after years apart. From then on, they'd never be apart.


- Brilliant writers and Brilliant users hail from different countries, but we need to use a single English dialect in our courses.

!!! tldr "Rule"
    For consistency, Brilliant lessons use the American spellings of words.
