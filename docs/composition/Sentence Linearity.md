# Sentence Linearity

!!!tldr "Rule"
    Sentences should advance a single thought, flowing from beginning to end.

The purpose of a **sentence** is to advance a complete thought. There's no magic point where a sentence needs to end, but the best sentences are direct and purposeful.

Avoid making qualifying statements in the middle of making a point. If this seems necessary, consider whether you've clearly established your assumptions.

!!! failure "No"
    The angles of a triangle, except for those on a curved manifold, sum to 180 degrees.

!!! Success "Yes"
    The angles of a triangle sum to 180 degrees.

</aside>

Establish the point early in each sentence. There are many silly ways that have been invented to start sentences with needless suspense. Outside the beginning of fairy tales, these are no good. They make the reader wonder what the sentence is going to be about as they read it.

!!! failure "No"
    Though it may be surprising, given how many sources of friction there are in real world systems, the sum of kinetic and potential energy is conserved.

!!! success "Yes"
    The sum of kinetic and potential energy is conserved, despite the many sources of friction in the real world.
