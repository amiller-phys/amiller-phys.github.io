# Lightweight Questions

!!!tldr "Rule"
        Lightweight questions are used for when the user doesn't yet have the tools to know the right answer or when we want them to guess.


- Lightweight questions **should** be used when
    - there is no right answer
    - at the current level of understanding, a true right answer can't be justified
    - the learner is supposed to take a guess
    - (or something more playful)
- **Don't** use them when
    - Envelope or another interactive solvable could be used instead, or
    - The learner has all the information they need to answer, and there is a single right answer — this is the canonical case for MCQs

- Don't say "take a guess, don't worry" when a lightweight choice appears. Instead, consistently use lightweight choice and full MCQ for separate purposes.

        Here are some wording suggestions:

        **Lightweight — a definite answer can't be established yet**

        - What kind of relationship might there be between M and x?
        - What do you think the relationship is between M and x?
        - What is a plausible relationship between M and x?
        - What do you expect the relationship between M and x could be?
        - What do you suspect the relationship between M and x could be?

        **MCQ — has a definite answer**

        - What is the relationship between M and x?
        - How are M and x related?

        **Don't do**

        - Guess the relationship between M and x.
        </aside>


They appear in a single lane, unless multiple questions refer to an image or interactive in the right lane.

- A lightweight question should have a succinct question statement.
- The question refers to an image that is directly above it.
- Setup text is placed above the image.
- This is a proper use of a lightweight question: the question is **not** expecting the learner to use knowledge from the lesson to answer.
- Lightweight questions do not have official solutions, but text after the question should say whether the learner is right or wrong, or state that the correct answer will be revealed later in the lesson.
